Turnip & Sunchoke Soup

I love the nuttiness you get from the sunchokes in this soup, and the turnips add just a little bit of sweetness along with their light vegetal flavor. The brioche and smoked salmon make this soup a bit of an indulgence, and the basil oil balances the profile with its assertive, herbaceous punch.

Ingredients

Quantity Yields 1.5 Qts

For the soup

  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 C sunchokes
  • 4 small hakurei turnips, diced
  • 4 C chicken stock
  • 5 Tbsp butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste

For the basil oil

  • 2 C tightly packed basil
  • Neutral oil to cover

For the brioche croutons

  • 1 inch thick slice brioche
  • 3 Tbsp butter
  • Small pinch salt

To finish

  • Soup
  • Croutons
  • Basil oil
  • 1 small tomato, large dice
  • 1 slice smoked salmon, torn
  • 5 parsley leaves

Quantity Yields 3 Qts

For the soup

  • 2 small onions, diced
  • 2 C sunchokes
  • 8 small hakurei turnips, diced
  • 8 C chicken stock
  • 10 Tbsp butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste

For the basil oil

  • 4 C tightly packed basil
  • Neutral oil to cover

For the brioche croutons

  • 2 inch thick slice brioche
  • 6 Tbsp butter
  • Small pinch salt

To finish

  • Soup
  • Croutons
  • Basil oil
  • 2 small tomatoes, large dice
  • 2 slices smoked salmon, torn
  • 10 parsley leaves

Quantity Yields 4.5 Qts

For the soup

  • 3 small onions, diced
  • 3 C sunchokes
  • 12 small hakurei turnips, diced
  • 12 C chicken stock
  • 15 Tbsp butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste

For the basil oil

  • 6 C tightly packed basil
  • Neutral oil to cover

For the brioche croutons

  • 3 inch thick slice brioche
  • 9 Tbsp butter
  • Small pinch salt

To finish

  • Soup
  • Croutons
  • Basil oil
  • 3 small tomatoes, large dice
  • 3 slices smoked salmon, torn
  • 15 parsley leaves

Procedure

For the soup

Melt 3/4 of the butter in a saucepan and add the sunchokes. Cook over low heat, shaking the pan frequently, until the sunchokes are a deep golden, and tender. Set aside. Sweat the onions in the remaining butter. Add turnips to the pan, cook for 1 minute and then add the stock and sunchokes, reserving the butter in the pan. Simmer until tender, then blend on high speed with reserved butter. Pass through a fine mesh strainer and season to taste. Chill in refrigerator.

For the basil oil

Combine the basil leaves in a blender with just enough oil to cover. Blend on high until completely homogenous. Strain twice through a fine mesh strainer, retaining solids in the strainer and straining oil through them on the second pass.

For the broiche croutons

Melt the butter in a skillet. Tear the broiche into bite sized pieces and add to the pan. Toast, stirring constantly, until croutons are golden and crispy. Lay out on a sheet tray, season, and allow to cool.

To finish

Using a ring mold, make a pile of tomato, salmon and croutons in the center of a chilled bowl. Pour the soup around the edges then remove the ring mold. Finish by tearing the parsley leaves and placing them on top, then drizzle the soup with a generous pour of basil oil. Note: this soup turns out to be delicious served chilled or hot.
